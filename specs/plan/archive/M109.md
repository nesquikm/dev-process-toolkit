## Notes

The original brainstorm design ‚Äî a tracked `.dpt/version.json` stamp with a bidirectional stored-versus-installed check ‚Äî was refuted by a four-way adversarial sweep on 2026-07-19 and deliberately dropped. The refutation is recorded in STE-394's Requirement so a future session does not revive it. Detection remains the sole authority on what changes; this milestone ships no new state.

Deferred, not rejected: extending probe #57 to read `user-invocable:` frontmatter (so the flag is governed rather than hand-maintained); widening the `splitMatch` regex so `CLAUDE.md:15` can use a taxonomically accurate word instead of the load-bearing "dispatch"; and an *offer* ‚Äî as opposed to a report ‚Äî at `/pr` or `/implement` Phase 5. The offer was cut because `/upgrade` refuses on a dirty tree, which is the state those surfaces almost always run in.

**Spec-time verification note.** A four-checker sweep over the first draft of these specs caught 11 defects, three of them substantive: the draft would have re-keyed nine *mid-run recovery* remedies ("commit or stash, then re-run") to point at read-only `/gate-check`, which cannot resume a half-applied migration; it cited a worked example in `docs/skill-anatomy.md` that does not exist (zero "upgrade" hits); and it asserted that a test currently reading `not.toMatch(/user-invocable:\s*false/)` would pass after the flip, when it inverts. The sweep also surfaced the latent probe #57 dead-leg now covered by AC-STE-394.8. The corrected specs are what this plan builds.
