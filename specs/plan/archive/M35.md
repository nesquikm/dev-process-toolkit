# M35 — Workflow Diagram II {#M35}

**Release target:** v1.36.1 (provisional patch). Codename TBD; finalize at `/ship-milestone`.
**Status:** drafted 2026-04-27 from `/brainstorm` decision (Approach 2 — phases kept, two-tier classDef styling, `/ship-milestone` elevated to spine).
**Origin:** post-M34 ship review — user spotted that `/implement orchestrates /tdd, /gate-check, /docs --quick, /pr` claim in the README is partially false and that the diagram treats all 14 skills as visual peers when only 5 carry the recommended path.

## Why this milestone exists

M34 (v1.36.0 "Compass") shipped a workflow diagram that achieved the "lifecycle at a glance" goal but missed two finer-grained signals: hierarchy (which skills are the spine vs auxiliary) and accuracy (the prose paragraph beneath the diagram makes three factually wrong claims about `/implement`). Both are README-visible to every new install. M35 is the corrective ship.

The classDef walk-back is deliberate: M34's "vanilla mermaid only" risk-scan call was conservative; GitHub's mermaid renderer has supported `classDef` since 2022. Spine vs secondary visual encoding is the lowest-effort hierarchy signal that doesn't require restructuring the four-phase subgraph layout.
