# M37 — Disambiguate {#M37}

**Release target:** v1.37.0 (provisional patch — doc-only, no public surface change). Codename "Disambiguate"; finalize at `/ship-milestone`.
**Status:** drafted 2026-04-27 from in-conversation discovery — a parallel `/spec-write` session walking § 0b top-to-bottom in tracker mode interpreted step 1 ("Mint a ULID via `Provider.mintId()`") as universal and proposed a `Provider.mintId()` call on a Linear-connected project, which is a structural violation per STE-85 (`TrackerProvider` does not implement `IdentityMinter`) and STE-76 / STE-110 (tracker-mode frontmatter forbids `id:`).

## Why this milestone exists

The toolkit's bimodal identity invariant is enforced *structurally* — `Provider.mintId()` on a `Provider`-typed value is a TypeScript error in tracker mode, and `buildFRFrontmatter(spec, trackerBinding)` throws if `spec.id` is also set. The runtime guarantees hold. But the user-facing prose in `/spec-write` § 0b reads as if step 1 were universal, and the "always local" qualifier on `Provider.mintId()` describes *where* minting happens, not *whether* it should happen at all. A tracker-mode operator walking the steps top-to-bottom can reach step 1 and propose the call, only to be caught by the type system or the helper invariant after wasted planning.

M37 closes the prose gap: step 1 is explicitly scoped to `mode: none`, and the tracker-mode reader is told what to skip and why. One file, one paragraph, no code change.
