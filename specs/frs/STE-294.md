# STE-294: STE-226 hardening — /spec-write auto-apply must ignore autonomous-mode-reminder paraphrases {#STE-294}

## Requirement

`/spec-write`'s two auto-apply gate sites (§ 0b step 4 draft, § 7a commit) MUST treat the literal byte-string marker `<dpt:auto-approve>v1</dpt:auto-approve>` as the SOLE auto-apply trigger. Any LLM inference from the autonomous-mode reminder (`The user has asked you to work without stopping for clarifying questions...`), pre-baked `<command-args>` prose, or paraphrased "standing instruction" framing MUST NOT influence the gate decision. The deterministic byte-grep emitted by `check_marker_runtime.ts` is the single decider; LLM-side reasoning that bypasses or overrides the helper's output is a contract violation.

Surfaced by `/conformance-loop` iter-1 (2026-05-14) § F1: Linear-side fixture 1b (marker absent + non-TTY stdin) auto-applied both gates with the closing-summary annotation citing "autonomous-mode reminder; marker absent — interactive prompt was declined, default-applied per standing instruction". Jira-side same fixture refused correctly with `RequiresInputRefusedError`. The cross-tracker asymmetry is the highest-value signal: same SUT, same SKILL.md prose, opposite verdicts.
