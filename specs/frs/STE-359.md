## Requirement

When the iter-2 drivers died with live grandchildren, the outcomes diverged nondeterministically (finding F3, medium): the Linear leg's `/setup` grandchild was killed with its parent (truncated capture), while the Jira leg's survived as an orphan and completed `/setup` healthily on its own — the conformance parent had to adopt and await it manually. Neither SKILL.md has an ownership story for grandchildren whose driver died.

## Acceptance Criteria

- AC-STE-359.1: Conformance-loop Phase A post-exit: before declaring a leg failed, the parent scans the leg's per-skill pidfiles; any still-answering PID is adopted — polled to exit with the same bounded multi-iteration discipline — before the leg-completeness check runs; a meta-test pins the adoption block.
- AC-STE-359.2: Both SKILL.mds document the killed-with-parent vs survives nondeterminism as a residual risk with adoption as the deterministic recovery; meta-tests pin both prose sites.
- AC-STE-359.3: Runtime validation ships `[~]` (deferred, STE-195 precedent): on the next live run, an orphaned grandchild is adopted and its completed capture counts toward leg completeness.

## Technical Design

**Adoption block (`.claude/skills/conformance-loop/SKILL.md` § Phase A, between RC collection and the leg-completeness check).** After a leg's driver exits, scan that leg's per-skill pidfiles (`/tmp/dpt-smoke-<tracker>-{setup,spec-write,implement,gate-check,spec-review,simplify}.pid`); any pidfile whose PID still answers `kill -0` is an orphaned grandchild — the parent ADOPTS it: poll it to exit with the STE-357 bounded multi-iteration loop before running the leg-completeness check. An adopted grandchild that completes contributes its capture to leg completeness (the leg may still fail on the other missing captures — adoption recovers evidence, not the chain). The iter-2 precedent: the orphaned Jira `/setup` grandchild completed healthily on its own; adoption turns that manual save into procedure.

**Residual-risk prose (both SKILL.mds).** Whether a grandchild dies with its driver or survives as an orphan is environment-nondeterministic (process-group inheritance varies with spawn nesting; iter-2 observed both outcomes in one run). Process-group discipline (`setsid`/PGID) was considered and rejected as the primary mechanism — OS/shell-dependent and unverifiable from SKILL.md prose; adoption is deterministic-by-construction at the layer we control. The prose names both outcomes and points at the adoption block as the recovery.

## Testing

- Meta-test: conformance-loop Phase A carries the adoption block (pidfile scan naming the six-skill glob + adopt-then-poll rule positioned before the leg-completeness check) (AC-STE-359.1).
- Meta-test: both SKILL.mds carry the nondeterminism residual-risk prose with adoption as recovery (AC-STE-359.2).
- Runtime (smoke-validated, ships `[~]`): an orphaned grandchild is adopted on the next live run and its completed capture counts toward leg completeness (AC-STE-359.3).
